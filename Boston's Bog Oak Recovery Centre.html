Boston's Bog Oak Recovery Centre

Boston, Lincolshire, UK

Home Portfolio About

Boston's Bog Oak Recovery Centre looks to manufacture modern products by combining recovered local bog wood and recycled plastic, utilising the river on the site for transport and trade, re-instating the historic trade links, which made Boston an influential town in the 13th century.

The project is designed around a steel structural frame that is expressed in the design. The envelope of the building is lightweight and de-constructable, allowing the building to be taken apart and it's parts to be used in new projects. The building design adapts to floods, the base of the building is split into 5 elements, which float when a flood occurs, these design features are part of a long-term strategy to adapt to rising sea levels.
