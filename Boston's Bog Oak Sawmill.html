Boston's Bog Oak Sawmill

Boston, Lincolshire, UK

Home Portfolio About

Boston's Bog Oak Sawmill, looks to identify and re-claim bog wood that lies in the Fenlands, once found the oak is recovered and de-barked, then transported to the site. The building is used predominately as a sawmill, preparing the timber for seasoning, the building works alongside Boston's Bog Oak Recovery Centre.

The building is designed around two concrete cores, acting to encourage stack effect and double up as wind turbines. The building uses a steel secondary structure, which acts to support the floating pods, that create the internals of the building. The building is designed to adapt to floods, the pods of the building use a concrete base, which floats through buoyancy, along with a rubber concertina, allowing for the lateral movement.
